Section 04

Sales Enablement & Messaging

Talking points and reference material for framing Healthcare Capital Strategies conversations with prospects.

Messaging is intended to frame Balefire as a partner that restores leadership's time and focus — not merely an investment manager.

Sales Literature — Pete's Suggestions

  1. "In today's environment, a strong balance sheet isn't just financial strength, it's what preserves independence and keeps decision-making in the hands of the community."
  2. "Hospitals aren't losing ground because of market conditions, they're losing because their capital strategy hasn't kept up."
  3. "The question for many boards is no longer whether to optimize capital strategy, but whether the current approach is sufficient to sustain independence."
  4. "CFOs and Boards are increasingly focused on strategic oversight, not administrative burden, making efficient plan investment management critical."
  5. "Hospitals don't hire Balefire Capital Strategies because they lack investment capability, they hired us because managing that piece internally was consuming a disproportionate amount of time and focus from leadership and the board."
  6. "Complexity is not a justification for inaction, especially when the financial impact of the status quo can reach tens of millions over time."

Sales Literature — Reference Article Quotes

These quotes are drawn from third-party industry articles referenced by the team for sales conversations. Source publications are not specified in the original notes.

  1. "...by 2029, healthcare assets managed by U.S.-based OCIOs are expected to grow at $388.4 billion, which is more than double the 2020 total of $116.2 billion."
  2. "The reason they hired us was not because they're bad at investing. They hired us because they have a problem with that piece of the puzzle — whether it extracts too much time from those that are running the operation (of the broader institution), or extracts too much time from the board of trustees or investment committee."
  3. "The CFO doesn't have the bandwidth to manage all the things that go around with the paperwork (and) the administrative work...their finance committees are really much more focused on making sure they have a strong balance sheet."
